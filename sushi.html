Sushi: My Favourite Food

Sushi has become a highly popular Japanese dish around the world. It's usually made up of strips of vegetables and meats (typically fish) wrapped in sushi rice and seaweed. these are known as sushi rolls, although another common form of sushi is 'onigiri,' which is a piece of seafood on top of some sushi rice, sometimes tied by a strip of seaweed (image below).

Why I Like Sushi

Sushi is among my list of favourite foods because I find that it's an interesting and delicious way seafood is prepared. Usually with seafood, steamboats or boiled prawns come to mind, but sushi is completely different. It also follows a few basic, straightforward ground instructions but can produce a great variety of different types of sushi. Whenever I find myself wanting to eat a cold dish that's still satisfying, I always turn to sushi.

How to Make Sushi

Below is a list of required ingredients for making a sushi roll.

  • Sushi rice
  • Unsalted dry seaweed
  • Vegetables of choice
  • Meat of choice
  • OPTIONAL: Rice vinegar, salt, sugar, condiments
